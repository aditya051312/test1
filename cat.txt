Cats are small, carnivorous mammals. Their native habitat is in the wild, but they have been domesticated by humans for thousands of years. Cats are often kept as pets for their companionship and ability to hunt vermin. They are agile and have sharp retractable claws. Cats use a variety of vocalizations and types of body language for communication, including meowing, purring, hissing, growling, squeaking, chirping, clicking, and grunting. Cats have a high breeding rate. Under controlled breeding, they can be bred and shown as registered pedigree pets. Cats have a keen sense of hearing and sight. They are known for their grooming behavior, spending a large portion of their waking hours cleaning themselves. Cats are known for being clean animals with their constant self-grooming. A group of cats is referred to as a clowder.
